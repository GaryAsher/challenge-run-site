1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that websites place on your device to store information. They help websites remember your preferences, keep you signed in, and understand how visitors use the site. Some cookies are essential for the site to function, while others help us improve your experience.

We also use similar technologies such as localStorage (browser-based storage) for saving your preferences and consent choices. These function similarly to cookies but are stored differently in your browser.

2. Cookies We Use

2.1 Essential Cookies

These cookies are necessary for the Website to function. They cannot be disabled. Without these cookies, services you have asked for (such as signing in) cannot be provided.

Name Provider Purpose Duration
sb-*-auth-token Supabase Keeps you signed in to your account. Stores your encrypted authentication session. 7 days
crc_theme CRC (localStorage) Remembers your selected theme (light/dark mode) and accent color so the page displays correctly on each visit. Persistent until cleared
crc_cookie_consent CRC (localStorage) Stores your cookie consent preferences so we do not ask you again on each visit. 1 year
cf_clearance Cloudflare Set by Cloudflare's security features (Turnstile challenge) to verify you are a legitimate visitor. 30 minutes

Legal basis: Legitimate interest — these cookies are strictly necessary to provide the service you requested.

2.2 Analytics Cookies

These cookies help us understand how visitors interact with the Website by collecting anonymized usage data. They are only set if you consent.

Name Provider Purpose Duration
Cloudflare Web Analytics Cloudflare Privacy-focused analytics that measures page views, referrers, and browser/device types. Cloudflare Web Analytics does not use client-side cookies, does not track individual users across sites, and does not collect personal data. However, it does send a beacon request from your browser. 2.3 Cookies We Do Not Use

For clarity, we do not use:

  • Advertising or marketing cookies
  • Cross-site tracking cookies
  • Social media tracking pixels
  • Cookies that sell or share your data with advertisers

4. Your Choices

4.1 Cookie Consent Banner

When you first visit the Website, a cookie consent banner appears at the bottom of the page. You can:

  • Accept All — enables essential and analytics cookies
  • Reject Non-Essential — enables only essential cookies
  • Manage — choose specific cookie categories

4.2 Changing Your Preferences

You can change your cookie preferences at any time by clicking Cookie Settings in the footer of any page.

4.3 Browser Controls

Most browsers allow you to control cookies through their settings. You can usually find these under "Privacy" or "Security" settings. Common options include:

  • Block all cookies
  • Block third-party cookies only
  • Delete cookies when you close your browser
  • Browse in private/incognito mode

Note: Blocking essential cookies will prevent you from signing in and may affect other functionality.

5. Do Not Track Signals

Some browsers send a "Do Not Track" (DNT) signal with each request. There is no universal standard for how websites should respond to DNT signals.

Our Website does not currently respond to DNT signals differently. However, our analytics service (Cloudflare Web Analytics) is already privacy-focused and does not track individuals across sites. If you prefer no analytics at all, you can reject analytics cookies using our consent banner.

6. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Cookie Policy from time to time to reflect changes in the cookies we use or for legal reasons. We will update the "Last updated" date at the top of this page.

If we add new categories of non-essential cookies, we will ask for your consent again.
